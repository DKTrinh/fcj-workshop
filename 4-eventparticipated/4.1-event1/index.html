Event 1

Summary Report: “Career Orientation & AWS Architecture (June 13)”

Event Objectives

  • Equip attendees with practical mindsets and career orientation amid fluctuating IT recruitment demands.
  • Understand the true nature of practical work for roles like Data Analytics Engineer and DevOps Engineer in enterprises.
  • Enhance core skills including critical thinking, communication, data storytelling, and problem-solving.
  • Build a personal development roadmap from a basic executor (Follower) to a System Thinker.
  • Discover corporate culture (No-Blame, Caring) and standard recruitment processes at multinational corporations (MNCs).
  • Explore growth opportunities through AWS community programs (First Cloud AI Journey, AWS Student Builder Group).

Speakers

  • Mr. Dat Pham – Data Analytics Engineer
  • Mr. Cuong Nguyen – Process Engineer
  • Mr. Danh Hoang Hieu Nghi – AI Engineer – AWS Community Builder – AWS Student Builder Group Leader
  • Mr. Trong H. Truong – DevOps Engineer @ Endava Vietnam
  • Mr. Kien & Mr. Tho – Speakers sharing architectural solutions

Key Highlights

Practical Data Analytics Work

  • Domain Differences: The Tech/Startup model (e.g., Kamereo) focuses on periodic reports, tracking trends, and finding root causes. The FMCG model (e.g., Colgate-Palmolive) targets machinery and IoT data projects to optimize production costs and drive long-term digital transformation.
  • 4 Core Skills: Critical thinking (digging deep into variance causes), Communication, Data Storytelling (turning dry numbers into meaningful operational/cost stories), and Problem Solving.

Career Growth Model

Guidance on elevating capabilities through 5 stages instead of chasing job titles:

  • Follower: Works based on detailed instructions, getting familiar with the environment and building foundational skills.
  • Learner: Understands solutions but still needs a mentor; proactively asks in-depth questions to accumulate practical experience.
  • Problem Solver: A crucial milestone where one proactively analyzes hard problems, proposes optimal solutions, and commits to output quality.
  • System Thinker: Sees the big picture, anticipates operational risks, assesses financial impacts, and optimizes systems long-term.
  • Super Star: Builds vision, comprehensive data strategy, and develops the next generation.

Corporate Culture & Recruitment at MNCs

  • Standard 4-step process: Screening & Initial Interview -> Capability Test -> Technical Interview (using the STAR model) -> Cultural Fit Assessment.
  • Cultural characteristics: No-Blame Post-Mortem culture (focusing on systemic root causes instead of blaming individuals) and Caring & Inclusive culture.
  • International lessons: Japan’s Wakon Yosai spirit and South Korean Chaebols’ strict discipline to meet global standards.

The True Nature of a DevOps Engineer

  • Reality vs. Myth: DevOps is not just about writing CI/CD pipelines or “fixing the system at midnight.” It requires systems thinking and automating repetitive tasks to optimize the entire team’s operational processes.
  • Core roadmap: Master Linux, Networking basics, programming languages (Python, Golang), and fully understand build, test, deploy, and log management processes.
  • Mindset: Learn to ask “Why” before “How”, identify the true owner of the problem, and use AI to leverage skills rather than as an excuse for mental laziness.

AWS Ecosystem & System Design

  • AWS Community: Long-term development roadmap through First Cloud AI Journey, AWS Student Builder Group, and AWS Community Builder.
  • Technical Case Study: Analyzing the design of a highly scalable URL Shortening Service deployed entirely on AWS cloud infrastructure.

Key Takeaways

Mindset & Career Orientation

  • Understand that personal growth lies in problem-solving capability and systems vision, not just in job titles.
  • Eliminate the illusion of “just copying and pasting commands,” replacing it with a deep understanding of core technologies.
  • Appreciate the “No-Blame” spirit, viewing mistakes as opportunities to patch system vulnerabilities.

Core Skills

  • Grasp the art of Data Storytelling: Reports are not just raw data, but must explain “Why the fluctuation?” and “What is the next action?”.
  • Improve interview skills using the STAR method to overcome rigorous MNC recruitment rounds.

Technical Knowledge

  • Grasp a clear roadmap to become a professional DevOps Engineer (Linux → Networking → Programming → CI/CD).
  • Visualize how to practically deploy a scalable serverless architecture (URL Shortener case study) on the AWS cloud.

Applying to Work

  • Change approach to reports/projects: Don’t just present data; dig deep to analyze causes and provide specific perspectives/solutions (applying Data Storytelling).
  • Reposition myself: Recognize that I am in the “Learner” stage, thereby practicing asking profound questions to advance to “Problem Solver.”
  • Shift debugging mindset: Apply the “No-Blame Post-Mortem” mindset in teamwork, focusing on logic flaws rather than blaming teammates.
  • Expand network: Proactively participate deeper in AWS Student Builder Group activities to connect with industry experts.

Event Experience

Attending the “Career Orientation & AWS Architecture” event gave me the most practical and profound insights into the current IT human resources market. Key experiences included:

Learning from practical experts

  • Honest sharing from Mr. Dat Pham and Mr. Trong Truong helped me shatter illusions about the Data and DevOps fields. Understanding the differences between domains (Startup vs FMCG) helped me identify the environment I want to aim for.

Experiencing systems thinking & processes

  • For the first time, I was deeply introduced to the 5-stage Career Growth model and the “No-Blame” culture. This completely changed how I view personal development: moving from someone who just follows instructions (Follower) to someone who sees the big picture (System Thinker).

Accessing practical architecture

  • The Case Study on the URL shortener architecture was very intuitive. Instead of just learning Cloud theory, I saw how AWS services integrate to solve real-world scalability problems.

Broadening vision and networking

  • The event helped me realize the importance of joining professional communities like AWS Builder. Soft skills like critical thinking, communication, and English are just as important as hard coding skills when aiming for MNCs.
